Email Enrichment Pilot — Key Findings & Recommendations

MSP US TAM · 20-account pilot · Tool benchmark

Objective
  • Ran a controlled pilot on 20 randomly selected MSP US accounts to benchmark email enrichment tools before scaling to the full TAM of 32,000 accounts.
Key findings
  • Apollo.io identified contacts at 80% of sampled accounts (16/20) and achieved a 78% professional email hit rate — the strongest single tool tested.
  • RocketReach found 3 professional emails that Apollo missed, making it a valuable gap-fill tool — not a primary replacement.
  • Clearbit was the weakest performer with no unique finds; it adds cost and complexity with zero incremental coverage and should be dropped.
  • For personal emails, People Data Labs outperformed RocketReach (50% vs 41% hit rate). RocketReach found the same emails for fewer people — no additional value for personal enrichment.
  • ~20% of accounts returned no contacts — a structural coverage gap to factor into TAM projections.
Recommended toolset
  • Professional email: Apollo.io (primary) → RocketReach on Apollo misses only. Expected hit rate: 87–94%.
  • Personal email: People Data Labs only. Expected hit rate: ~50% of identified contacts.
  • Drop: Clearbit and RocketReach for personal email — both are redundant in the recommended stack.
Scale projection (32,000 accounts)
  • ~25,600 accounts expected to yield contacts (based on 80% pilot coverage rate).
  • Estimated ~51,200 contactable people at an average of 2 contacts per account.
  • Negotiate bulk API pricing with Apollo and PDL before full run. Limit RocketReach spend to gap-fill only to control costs.
Next steps before full rollout
  • Validate deliverability of the 3 RocketReach-exclusive work emails from the pilot.
  • Confirm CAN-SPAM compliance posture for personal email outreach.
  • Tag email source in CRM to enable post-send deliverability tracking by tool.
  • Build deduplication layer to prevent duplicate records across tool outputs.
